# :material-rocket-launch: Next Level Python Tasks

These tasks combine the skills from:

- :material-monitor: Outputs, inputs and variables
- :material-database-outline: Data types and arithmetic

!!! note "Important Information before you Begin"

    You do not need to record your results for these tasks.

    This is a test in resilience: try things, fix errors, and keep improving your code.

    > Remember, we learn through making mistakes.

---

## :material-tools: Rules for These Tasks

For every task:

- Use clear variable names.
- Ask the user for input where needed.
- Convert numbers using `int()` or `float()`.
- Display answers with helpful messages.
- Test your program with more than one set of inputs.

!!! warning "Important"

    These tasks only use the skills from lessons 1 and 2.

    You do not need `if`, `else` or loops yet.

---

## :material-format-list-checks: Challenge Tasks

!!! question "Start where you feel confident, then try the next level."

    === "Level 1"

        **Task 1: Personal Receipt**

        Ask the user for:

        - Their name
        - The name of an item
        - The price of the item

        Display a short receipt.

        **Example output:** `Alex bought a keyboard for £24.99`

        **Task 2: Minutes Converter**

        Ask the user to enter a number of hours.

        Convert the hours into minutes.

        **Formula:** `minutes = hours * 60`

        **Example output:** `3 hours is 180 minutes`

    === "Level 2"

        **Task 3: Game Score Total**

        Ask the user for:

        - Their player name
        - Their score in round 1
        - Their score in round 2
        - Their score in round 3

        Calculate and display their total score.

        **Extension:** Also display their average score.

        **Task 4: Shopping Basket**

        Ask the user for:

        - The price of item 1
        - The price of item 2
        - The price of item 3

        Calculate and display the total cost.

        Then add a delivery charge of `2.50`.

        Display the final amount to pay.

    === "Level 3"

        **Task 5: Cinema Trip Calculator**

        A cinema ticket costs `7.50`.

        Popcorn costs `3.25`.

        A drink costs `2.10`.

        Ask the user for:

        - Their name
        - How many tickets they want
        - How many popcorns they want
        - How many drinks they want

        Calculate and display:

        - Ticket total
        - Snack total
        - Final total

        **Task 6: Room Paint Estimator**

        Ask the user for:

        - The width of a wall in metres
        - The height of a wall in metres
        - The cost of paint per square metre

        Calculate and display:

        - The area of the wall
        - The total paint cost

        **Formula:** `area = width * height`

    === "Level 4"

        **Task 7: Fitness Tracker**

        Ask the user for:

        - Their name
        - Steps walked on Monday
        - Steps walked on Tuesday
        - Steps walked on Wednesday
        - Steps walked on Thursday
        - Steps walked on Friday

        Calculate and display:

        - Total steps
        - Average steps per day
        - Total distance walked

        Use this estimate:

        ```text
        1 step = 0.0008 kilometres
        ```

        **Task 8: Party Planner**

        Ask the user for:

        - Number of guests
        - Cost of food per guest
        - Cost of drinks per guest
        - Room hire cost

        Calculate and display:

        - Total food cost
        - Total drinks cost
        - Room hire cost
        - Final party cost
        - Cost per guest

    === "Challenge"

        **Task 9: Mini Budget Planner**

        Create a program that helps someone plan a weekly budget.

        Ask the user for:

        - Their name
        - Weekly income
        - Food cost
        - Travel cost
        - Entertainment cost
        - Savings amount

        Calculate and display:

        - Total spending
        - Money left after spending
        - Money left after spending and savings
        - A clear budget summary using the user's name

        **Extra challenge:** Ask for one more spending category of your choice and include it in the calculations.

        **Task 10: Theme Park Cost Calculator**

        A group is visiting a theme park.

        Ask the user for:

        - Group name
        - Number of child tickets
        - Number of adult tickets
        - Number of meal deals
        - Travel cost

        Use these prices:

        | Item | Price |
        |-----------|-----------|
        | Child ticket | `14.50` |
        | Adult ticket | `22.00` |
        | Meal deal | `6.75` |

        Calculate and display:

        - Child ticket total
        - Adult ticket total
        - Meal deal total
        - Travel cost
        - Final group cost
        - Average cost per person

## :material-lightbulb-on: Challenge Tips

- Build one part at a time.
- Test each calculation before adding the next one.
- Use `float()` for prices and measurements.
- Use `int()` for whole numbers such as people, tickets or scores.
- Use comments to explain important parts of your code.
