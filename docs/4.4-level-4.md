# :material-numeric-4-circle: Assessment Level 4

Level 4 checks that you can combine inputs, variables, number conversion and selection to solve a more challenging problem.

---

## :material-sort-numeric-ascending: Task: Order Three Numbers

Create a program that:

- Asks the user to type in three numbers.
- Stores each number in a variable.
- Displays the three numbers in order.
- Starts with the smallest number.

Example:

```text
Enter the first number: 8
Enter the second number: 3
Enter the third number: 12
The numbers in order are 3, 8, 12
```

---

## :material-check-circle-outline: Your Program Must

- Use three separate inputs.
- Convert each input into an integer.
- Use selection to compare the numbers.
- Display the numbers from smallest to largest.
- Work with different sets of numbers.

---

## :material-flask-outline: Test Data

Test your program with at least three different sets of numbers.

| Test | Numbers Entered | Expected Order |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|
| 1 | `8`, `3`, `12` | `3`, `8`, `12` |
| 2 | `20`, `5`, `10` | `5`, `10`, `20` |
| 3 | `1`, `9`, `4` | `1`, `4`, `9` |

---

## :material-camera-outline: Evidence

Submit a screenshot or copy of your code.

Make sure it is cropped and large enough to read.

---

## :material-lightbulb-on: Hints

- Solve one comparison at a time.
- Think carefully about every possible order.
- Test your program after each change.
- Check that your indentation matches your `if` and `else` statements.
